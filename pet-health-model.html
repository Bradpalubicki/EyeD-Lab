IP Strategy — The Iris Advantage

Nose print is crowded and patented. Iris is open.

Critical Finding: The iSciLab Patent Blocks Nose Print — Not Iris
iSciLab holds US Patent #11,019,250 (2021): "Method for implementing animal nose pattern biometric identification system on mobile devices" — active in US, EU, Canada, Japan. Any commercial nose print product must license from them or design around the patent. Animal iris recognition has no dominant blocking patent. The foundational Daugman human iris patents expired 2011–2016. Smartphone-based animal iris recognition appears to be open patent territory. This is EyeD ID's moat: build iris, not nose print, and the field is clear.
The ML Challenge Is Solvable in 3–6 Months
Neither iOS Vision nor Google ML Kit works on animal irises out of the box. But fine-tuning on 5,000–10,000 labeled animal iris images using a pretrained transformer backbone (EfficientNet, ViT) is technically feasible in 3–6 months with a research partner. The cat slit-pupil challenge (pupil varies 1mm–14mm in different light) is addressed by pupil normalization algorithms — proven in academic research to restore recognition accuracy to 99%+ even with significant dilation. Dog motion blur is solved by burst-capture + best-frame selection. These are engineering problems, not fundamental barriers.
