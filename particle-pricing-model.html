RPM Reimbursement ROI — Why Signal Pays for Itself 98x Over
Signal enables clinics to bill Medicare for Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM). Per-patient reimbursement:

CPT 99454 (device supply/data transmission): ~$47.00/patient/month
CPT 99457 (RPM treatment management, first 20 min): ~$51.77/patient/month
Total RPM reimbursement: ~$98.77/patient/month

Our Particle cost at current tier: $1.00/patient/month
ROI: $98.77 reimbursement on $1.00 cost = 98:1 return.

At the 100K tier ($0.30 PMPM), the ROI jumps to 329:1. This is the single strongest selling point for Standard+ clinics: Signal doesn't cost them money — it makes them money.

Flat JSON — Available Now
Developer-friendly structured JSON. Data grouped by category: Allergies, Medications, Labs, Problems, Encounters, Immunizations, Procedures, Vital Signs. Our particle-health.ts library is already built for this format.

Industry-standard healthcare data format. Richer structured data with standard codes and resource references. Useful for interop with other systems. Additive — Flat stays stable when FHIR comes online.

Decision: Basic Tier = Query-Only (No Signal)
Original problem: Basic ($99/mo) with 200 Signal patients would cost us $200/mo in Particle fees — a $101/mo loss per clinic. Fix applied: Basic tier provides query-only access (Workbench Gold COGS). Signal real-time monitoring requires Standard ($499/mo) or above. This preserves the $99 entry point for small clinics that just need record lookups, while reserving the RPM-enabling Signal product for tiers where the margin works. Standard clinics earn ~$98.77/patient/month in RPM reimbursement — our $499/mo fee is a rounding error on their new revenue.
The 85% Margin Zone
At 100K+ patients ($0.30 PMPM) with Enterprise pricing ($2K-10K/mo per network), our gross margin hits 85%. This is the same margin structure as mature SaaS. The path: land 5 Standard clinics (break even on Particle), then grow into Enterprise deals where the margin explodes.
