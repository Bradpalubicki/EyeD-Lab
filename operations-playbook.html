Phase 1: The Patient Flowmodel

Launch Everywhere — Day 1 — All 48 Clinics

What it is: A one-time data check that happens the exact minute a patient walks through the door. No long-term tracking. No devices. No new software.

Front Desk The Gatekeepers

What you do: Check the patient in exactly like you do now. When the digital check-in tablet asks for permission to fetch their records, make sure the patient signs it.

What to say if they ask: "This safely searches Texas medical networks for your allergies and past prescriptions so your doctor can treat you safely today — without making you fill out a massive paper packet."

The technology: Clicking "Arrived" in eCW automatically forces Particle Health to search 160,000 hospitals and pharmacies. It dumps their entire history into their chart in under 12 seconds.

Providers MDs, NPs, PAs

What you do: Open the eCW chart. Look at the new "External Documents" tab before you walk into the room. Use that history to make your treatment decisions.

The mandatory step: Before you close the chart, type .ECW_DATA_REVIEW into your progress note. This shortcut text legally proves to the insurance company that you reviewed outside files.

The financial payoff: Typing that macro securely upgrades the visit level. Insurance automatically pays us $33 to $39 more per visit because we did the extra work of reviewing outside records.

Billing Centralized Billing Team

What you do: Run the electronic "Claim Scrubber" engine daily.

The compliance check: If a biller tries to send a high-paying claim, the software checks for two things:

1. Did the provider use the .ECW_DATA_REVIEW macro?

2. Is there an actual data transaction timestamp from Particle Health?

If yes to both → send it. If no to either → bounce it back. No exceptions.

Phase 2: Behavioral Health Expansion

3 Pilot Clinics First — ~1,000 Patients

What it is: Integrating mental health care (ADHD, Anxiety, Depression, PTSD) with physical care.

The Safety Rule

The Risk: Many of our patients take testosterone or have high blood pressure, but they also get ADHD stimulants (like Adderall) from outside psychiatrists. Mixing these causes severe cardiovascular risks. Patients routinely forget — or deliberately omit — these prescriptions.

The Action: Providers must check Particle's medication logs to spot hidden outside psychiatric prescriptions before ordering any new drugs.

The Collaborative Care (CoCM) Workflow

Step 1: The primary doctor refers a struggling patient to our internal Centralized Care Manager.

Step 2: The Care Manager calls the patient monthly, runs mental health checks (like the PHQ-9 depression scale), and updates a master registry.

Step 3: A 1099 Virtual Psychiatrist (hired remotely) logs in weekly, reviews that registry, and types care recommendations directly into eCW.

The billing payoff: This team-based approach allows the centralized billing hub to compliantly bill $63 to $131 per month per patient under specialized care coordination codes (CoCM + BHI).

Phase 3: Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM)

Scale to 500 Patients — 5 Pilot Clinics — Ramp 3-5%/Month

What it is: Tracking a patient's physical vital signs (heart rate, blood pressure, SpO2) from home using technology they already own.

The Apple Watch Solution (No Hardware Investment)

The old way: Ordering and shipping expensive medical hardware to a patient's house, which they frequently lose, break, or forget to charge.

The new way: If the patient owns an Apple Watch (Series 9 or newer), the Care Manager pairs it directly to eCW via the Apple HealthKit API. The watch tracks heart rate, ECG, blood oxygen, and AFib detection — all streaming automatically.

The 16-Day Data Rule (CPT 99454 — $58/month)

The strict law: We cannot collect the $58 monthly device fee from insurance unless the patient's watch automatically streams health data on at least 16 separate days out of a 30-day month.

The staff action: The software dashboard tracks this automatically. If a patient drops off and hasn't synced their watch in 4 days, a Care Manager must text them to prompt a manual sync. If they hit 15 days or fewer, the billing hub will strip the code to prevent an audit.

The 20-Minute Time Log (CPT 99457 — $69/month)

The law: Clinical staff must spend a minimum of 20 distinct, un-duplicated minutes per month reviewing a patient's incoming device data and communicating with them about their progress.

The rule: You must use the built-in eCW stopwatch timer. General office visits, standard phone tag, or manual time estimates do not count. The timer creates an electronic time log that serves as your audit evidence.
